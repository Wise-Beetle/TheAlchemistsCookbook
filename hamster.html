How to power Tokyo with hamsters

A very quick and simple recipe that is easy to understand

Syrian hamster stats:

  • Need 800 in2 of space
  • They need an enclosure that's 24 in tall
  • They run on a wheel for roughly 5 hours a day

Tokyo stats:

  • Uses 220 million kWh (kilowatt-hour) a day

Step 1) Calculate hamster energy output

So we have to calculate mechanical power now.

Pmech = τ x ω

Mechanical power = Torque x Angular Velocity (in rad/s)

Torque = Force x Radius

Force = 0.2 (Average Hamster force)

Radius = 5 in (radius based on 10 inch hamster wheel)

τ = 0.2 x 5 = 1

Angular Velocity = RPM (revolutions per minute) x 2 π / 60

RPM = 60 (The wheel spins 60 times a second)

ω = 60 x 2 π / 60 = 6.28

Pmech = 1 x 6.28 = 6.28

Now to calculate the energy of one hamster each day…

We use this formula to calculate energy in joules.

E = P x t x η

Energy = Power x time in seconds x system efficiency

t = 18,000 (18,000 seconds in one day)

η = 0.4 (Average energy loss)

And as we just found out…

P = 6.28

E = 6.28 x 18,000 x 0.4 = 45,216

So each hamster generates roughly 45,216 joules of electricity a day.

Or to figure out the watt-hours, we divide that number by 3,600.

wH of each hamster a day = 12.56

Step 2) Estimate needed hamster quantities

We have now deduced the energy output of a single hamster of the course of 24 hours, in the form of watt-hours. We also know that Tokyo uses 220 million kilowatt hours a day, so we are able to calculate a near perfect amount of hamsters we would need to successfully power Tokyo. I think…

You also must know that one kilowatt-hour = 1000 watt-hours.

So we multiply Tokyo's kilowatt-hours a day by 1000 to get its daily watt-hours.

220 million x 1,000 = 220 billion

So Tokyo uses 220 billion watt-hours a day, and one hamster only generates 12.

But we’re good. It's all good.

Now we do the math, its simple I promise:

220 billion total / 12.56 per hamster

So we need a grand total of 17,515,923,567 hamsters
