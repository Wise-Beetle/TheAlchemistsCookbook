How to make diamonds from pizza.

(In 5 days rather than 5 million years)

Things to know:

  • Every organic molecule (like pizza) has carbon
  • Diamonds are the purest form of compressed carbon
  • Diamonds are naturally formed in earth's mantle, where it's around 1,300-1,600 degrees celsius and about 5- 6 gigascapels of pressure (equivalent to balancing an airplane on your finger)

Step 1) Make the carbon

  1. Stuff your pizza inside the paint can, The more pizza you use the more diamonds you'll get, but any estimates after this point are based on a 14”, 900g large cheese pizza.
  2. Seal the paint can with the lid, then drill a hole in the top for the gasses to escape.
  3. Place your can in a roaring fire. Only remove it once the smoke exits the can has gone from white (moisture) to yellow-ish grey (organics) then to no smoke at all.
  4. Remove from the fire. Once the smoke and gas has stopped, remove from the fire and let it cool for 4.5 hours.

Optional extra step (purify)

  1. Keep the charcoal in the same can, and place it back into a calmer fire.
  2. Put small amounts of water on the fire, you are creating steam that purifies and etches pores into the carbon.

Calculations

Using the formulas below we can calculate how much carbon one large pizza will contain.

Wdry = Wraw x (1 - MC% / 100)

(Or in plain terms: dry weight is equal to the weight of the pizza multiplied by one minus the moisture content percentage divided by 100)

One large pizza = 900g

Moisture content of one large pizza = 45%

Wdry = 495g

But that's not all.

We use this formula to calculate the biochar yield efficiency (how much is burned off)

Wbiochar = Wdry x Ybiochar% / 100

One large pizza = 495g of dry weight

27% of that is burned off

Wbiochar = 133.65g

But, that's still not all…

Biochar is not 100% pure carbon, so we use this formula after purifying:

Wcarbon = Wbiochar x FC% / 100

Fixed carbon percentage = FC% = approximately 70%

Wcarbon = 93.56g

So we end up with about 93.56g of pizza carbon, about 10.4% of our initial pizza mass…

… But that's still plenty to make the diamonds with.

Step 2) Make the graphite

Before we get to the diamonds we must turn the carbon into graphite (yes, what pencils are made of)

So I hope you got your graphitization furnace unboxed, but let's get to it.

  1. Put your purified carbon into the graphite boat, then place the boat inside the chamber onto the graphite heath support.
  2. Turn on the vacuum in the chamber to remove all oxygen from the chamber, or optionally fill the chamber with argon gas.
  3. Slowly bring the temperature to 2,800° C
  4. Once at full temperature, the graphitization process only takes roughly 30 minutes to an hour.
  5. Do not open until cooled, or everything will blow up or ignite.

I’ll use this rough formula to calculate the losses:

Wgraphite = Wcarbon x 0.95

Wgraphite = 88.88g

Such a nice number huh?

Step 3) Make the diamonds

Finally! We can finally make our diamonds!

We have to do eight batches for a reason, if you do it in one big batch it would be like cooking all of your cookie dough in one big ball. It will give you a big brown ugly mess that's worth nothing. No offense to ugly diamonds.

Let's do it!

  1. Split the graphite into eight batches of 11.11g (still a nice number haha)
  2. Place your diamond sliver on the base of your cubic press, and dump one part of your graphite on top.
  3. Put an equal amount of iron in the machine, this will be the catalyst that the melted, dissolved graphite will move through
  4. Close and turn on your cubic press.
  5. Warning, things may:
    • Melt
    • Blowup
    • Explode
    • Implode
    • Create a citywide power outage

But it will all be worth it for your diamonds.

  1. If everything goes right, you should be making diamonds.

The carbon will precipitate through the molten iron and then deposit onto the diamond seed (the sliver).

Over the next few days or weeks the seed will gradually grow a new diamond

  1. Once the diamond is finished, you must slowly cool down and depressurize the press.
  2. After everything is done, you should have a whole diamond at the bottom of the chamber
  3. Repeat steps 2 through 8 until you have no graphite left.
